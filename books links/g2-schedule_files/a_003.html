                            Aloha! Thank you for this excellent write up! The visualizations are awesome. One point...is it entirely true that the axes have no physical significance? I think that actually depends upon the situation, doesn't it? For example, in chemical engineering, many "dimensionless" quantities are used to simplify understanding of physical phenomena. These include things such as Prandtl number (ratio of momentum diffusivity to thermal diffusivity), Reynolds number (ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces within a fluid), Schmidt number (ratio of momentum diffusivity (viscosity) and mass diffusivity). If I'm not mistaken, these groupings would be the principal components that come out of a PCA on a body of data containing observations of the ratio'ed features, and since they are ratios of physical properties of the system, they themselves have a physical significance, i.e., how much is the system dominated by one thing over the other thing.
